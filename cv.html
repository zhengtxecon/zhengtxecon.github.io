Hello, I am Tianxiang Zheng (郑天翔)

I am a 3rd-year PhD candidate in Economics at University College London with research interests in labor economics, technological change, and economic development. My work applies advanced computational methods to understand how automation and AI impact labor markets across different economic contexts.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in labour and development economics. I am particularly interested in how technology innovation and adoption shape organizational structures and individual decision-making processes, and their consequences in developing countries.

In addition to my primary interests, I explore topics such as urban economics and the pervasive impact of behavioral heuristics and social norms on inequality and biases within society.

Academic Leadership

I am currently the co-organizer of the Applied Economics Reading Group at UCL. This student-led reading group provides a platform for insightful discussions on cutting-edge research papers, fostering an environment of constructive feedback and intellectual exchange.

If you share an interest in attending or presenting at our sessions, please feel free to reach out to me directly via email.
